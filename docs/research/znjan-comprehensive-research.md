## 1. OVERVIEW AND LOCATION

**Znjan** (Croatian: Znjan) is both a city district and the largest beach in Split, Croatia's second-largest city. It is located in the eastern part of Split, stretching over **2 kilometers** along the Adriatic coast.

- **GPS Coordinates:** Approximately 43.50 N, 16.48 E
- **Distance from Diocletian's Palace (Old Town):** ~4 km east, roughly 15 minutes by car or 10-15 minutes by Uber (EUR 6-9)
- **Beach type:** Pebble, gravel, and rocky sections -- not sandy
- **Nickname since renovation:** "The New Miami of Split" -- due to white stone promenades, palm trees, and modern architecture
- **Total concession area:** 218,322 square meters (95,176 m2 marine + 123,146 m2 land)

## 2. THE EUR 45M TRANSFORMATION PROJECT

### Core Facts
| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Total investment** | EUR 45.5 million |
| **Project scale** | Largest urban development in Split in 45+ years |
| **Construction start** | February 2024 |
| **Construction duration** | Exactly 365 days |
| **Beaches opened early** | Mid-May 2025 |
| **Grand opening** | June 21, 2025 (first day of summer) |
| **Opening ceremony name** | "Ca ce mi Copacabana" (after the famous Oliver Dragojevic song) |
| **Attendance at grand opening** | ~10,000 people |

### Financing
- City of Split municipal budget
- European Investment Bank (EIB) loan
- Integrated Territorial Programme (ITP) funds
- Approximately EUR 14 million in non-repayable EU grants

### What Was Built
- **1.7 km promenade** with dedicated cycling path (from Zenta Marina to Duilovo)
- **Underground garage** with 530 parking spaces
- **451 outdoor parking spaces** (~1,000 total vehicle capacity)
- **11 designated hospitality venue locations** (restaurants, bars, cafes)
- **Central amphitheater** for concerts and cultural events
- **Children's playgrounds**, splash pads, climbing frames
- **Sports courts** (basketball, mini-football, beach volleyball, tennis, cageball)
- **Accessible beach** with ramps, sea-entry lift, and free adapted wheelchair
- **Diving board/platform**
- **48,000 m2 of green space** -- second-largest green zone in Split after Marjan Forest Park
- **800+ new trees**, 730 trees and palms, 12,815+ native shrubs and perennials
- Free public showers and changing cabins at regular intervals
- Modern public restrooms

### Pre-Renovation History
Before the transformation, the Znjan plateau was widely described as a "dusty plateau filled with cars and makeshift cafes" -- known locally as a "graveyard of wild cafes." Despite being Split's largest beach, it was considered the "ugly duckling" of the city's coastline. The plateau was originally created/expanded for the historic 1998 visit of Pope John Paul II.

## 3. HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE: POPE JOHN PAUL II VISIT

On **October 4, 1998**, Pope John Paul II celebrated a Solemn Mass at the Znjan plateau commemorating the 1,700th anniversary of Split. Key facts:

- Approximately **500,000 faithful** attended
- 50,000 attendees came from Bosnia-Herzegovina; thousands more from Germany
- The altar was shaped like a clipper ship with sails
- 600 priests concelebrated; 47 bishops and cardinals were in attendance
- President Franjo Tudman and top government officials attended
- A specially composed song honoring this visit was performed at the 2025 grand opening

## 5. BEACH AREAS AND ZONES

The beach complex consists of several distinct zones:

### Main Beach Areas (Znjan 1 and Znjan 2)
- Also known as "Saint John Paul II Beach"
- Pebble and gravel surface
- Primary swimming and sunbathing areas
- Gradual slope into the sea in most sections (family-friendly)
- Divided into several lagoons by a stone pier

### Children's / Family Zone
- Playgrounds with climbing frames
- Splash pads
- Inflatable water park (~1,000 m2 platform with slides and trampolines, seasonal from approximately June 1)
- Dedicated areas with shallow water entry

### Sports Zone
- Basketball courts
- Mini-football (5-a-side) pitches
- Tennis courts
- Beach volleyball courts
- Cageball

### Promenade
- 1.7 km palm-lined walkway
- Dedicated bicycle path
- Ideal for running, cycling, and evening strolls
- Connects Zenta Marina (west) to Duilovo (east)

### Amphitheater Area
- Central open-air venue
- Hosts concerts, cultural events, and performances
- Used for the grand opening concert (Ante Gelo + 17 guest performers)

### Accessible Beach
- Located east of the main beaches
- Two ramps with incline for easier entrance to the sea
- Lift for entering the sea
- Special stairs
- Free wheelchair adapted for sea entry available

### Dog Beach
- Located at the far eastern end (Duilovo area)
- Past the main Znjan zone
- Pebble beach with shower, trash cans, benches, and a changing cabin

### Green Zone
- 48,000 m2 of landscaped greenery
- 800+ trees and palms
- 12,815+ native shrubs and perennials
- Second-largest green area in Split after Marjan

## 14. VISITOR TIPS (SYNTHESIZED FROM REVIEWS)

1. **Arrive early** in peak season (July-August) -- it gets crowded despite its large size
2. **Bring water shoes** -- the beach is pebble/gravel, not sand; rocky entry points can be sharp
3. **Bring your own towel** to save on sunbed rental costs (EUR 15-50)
4. **Use bus Line 8 or 15** from the city center -- convenient and affordable (~EUR 1.50)
5. **The underground garage** is the easiest parking option for drivers
6. **Watch depth** -- the water deepens quickly in some areas, not always gradual
7. **Sunset walks** along the 1.7 km promenade are highly recommended
8. **Bring snorkeling gear** -- the clear water with abundant fish makes it excellent for snorkeling
9. **Dog owners** should head to the far eastern end (Duilovo) for the designated dog beach
10. **Expect premium prices** at beach bars -- a Coke is ~EUR 5, cappuccino ~EUR 3.60
